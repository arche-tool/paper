The best results are obtained when only orientations in the immediate vicinity of the GBs are considered for further calculations. Figure \ref{657394} compares the optimized OR (using both average grain orientation and near-boundary orientations) for the Fe-Ni 29% sample with different theoretical ORs. As it can be seen, the near-boundary algorithm provides by far the lowest angular deviation (cf. Figure \ref{657394}(right)) while the average orientation approach exhibits similar characteristics to the K-S OR, both in terms of average angular deviation and orientation transformation.
