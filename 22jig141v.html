The best results are obtained when only orientations in the immediate vicinity of the GBs are considered for further calculations. Figure \ref{657394} compares the optimized OR using both average grain orientation (optimized (avg)) and near-boundary orientations (optimized (GB)) for the Fe-29%Ni sample with different theoretical ORs. As it can be seen, the near-boundary algorithm provides by far the lowest angular deviation (cf. Figure \ref{657394}(right)) while the average orientation approach exhibits similar characteristics to the K-S OR, both in terms of average angular deviation and the relationship between the closed packed planes/directions in parent and product phases (cf. Figure \ref{657394}(left)) .
