Validation and discussion

Figure \ref{460293}a shows the results of the presented reconstruction method after different cycles of iteration. These microstructures are a subset of the reconstructed parent structure which correspond to the marked areas in Figure \ref{406044}. As it can be seen, after the first iteration, some reconstructed grains incorporate more than one parent grain. This can be also understood by taking the average angular deviation (\(\Delta g\)) for each reconstructed grain into consideration (cf. Figure \ref{460293}b). High angular deviation of the large reconstructed austenite grains indicates the presence of more than one parent grain in the cluster. Only two steps of iterations is enough to quickly refine these grains into smaller austenite grains with sufficiently low angular deviations. However, the algorithm fails to reconstruct some small features of the microstructure (especially crystal twinnings) at this level of refinement (cf. the missed twins marked ).  However, the reconstructed microstructure does not significantly change after 7 steps of iterations.
