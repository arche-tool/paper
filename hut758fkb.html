The complete orientation map of the reconstructed parent austenite phase is presented in Figure \ref{433657}. Self-evidently, this microstructure is very similar to the experimental results shown in Figure \ref{406044}a, both in terms of grain size/ morphology and crystallographic orientations. A unique aspect of the presented reconstruction method is that unlike the other alternative techniques (e.g. the ARPGE model based on the group properties of ferrite variants of the same parent austenite developed by Cayron et al \cite{cayron2007arpge}) which produces a high number of unsolved points, the algorithm presented in this paper is able to find a unique solution as parent phase orientation for every data points of the product phase. The latter is a consequence of employing the Markov Clustering algorithm for the reconstruction purpose. A cross-correlation based approach was utilized to deterministically validate the reconstructed microstructure. To this purpose, the misorientation angle between corresponding data points in the experimentally obtained and the reconstructed microstructures (i.e. the microstructures presented in Figure \ref{406044}a and Figure \ref{433657}, respectively) was calculated.
