Characterization of the austenite phase at high temperatures is important for understanding the microstructural evolution during steel processing. The austenite phase can be reconstructed from the room-temperature microstructure based on the orientation relationship between the parent and product phases. The actual orientation relationships in steels are often often based on the well-known correspondence relations (e.g. Kurdjumov-Sachs) which may differ from the experimentally observed correspondences. This work introduces a new approach aiming to improve the current state-of-the-art in prior phase reconstruction. It consists of two new methods, one to automatically identify the optimum orientation relationship and another one to reconstruct the parent phase microstructure.
