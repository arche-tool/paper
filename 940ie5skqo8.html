Orientation relationship calculation

As mentioned before, the presence of a specific orientation relationship between parent and product phases is one of the main characteristics of the martensitic transformation. Each parent phase reconstruction method uses an OR as the input for its calculation, therefore, to achieve the best possible reconstruction one has to provide an accurate OR value. Despite the proposed theoretical ORs such as K-S or N-W correspondences for steels, a more accurate OR can only be derived from experimental data. The available methods for OR identification \cite{germain2007determination,miyamoto2009accurate} depend on the manual selection of parent grains, which is time-consuming and in some cases, depending on the microstructure, impractical.
The proposed algorithm has an optional stage where it can derive the OR from the pre-processed microstructure graph in a fully automated manner based only on the misorientation between neighbouring grains. When analyzing the microstructure of a transformed sample, one can observe two kinds of grain boundaries. The first one is between a parent phase and product phase grains, when still a residual fraction of the parent phase is present in the transformed microstructure, and the second one is between product phase grains. On both kinds, there is another level of differentiation but not observable directly on the microstructure graph (this is a task for the next stage i.e. the reconstruction algorithm). A grain boundary of the first kind could be between a retained parent grain and a product grain produced by the same parent grain, in such a case the misorientation is expected to match \(T^{\alpha'\leftrightarrow\gamma}\) after taking symmetry in consideration, as described in Equation \ref{eq:1}. If the product grain is produced from a different parent, no assumption can be made about its misorientation. A similar differentiation also occurs in the second kind boundary, if two product grains are coming from different parents, as in the previous kind, nothing is assumed. However, if both product grains were derived from the same parent then they also hold an orientation relationship. But in this case, it is given by the double relati of the OR between parent and product grains (\(T_m^{\alpha'\leftrightarrow\gamma}T_n^{\alpha'\leftrightarrow\gamma}\)) as described by Equation \ref{eq:2}, after taking symmetry in consideration.
