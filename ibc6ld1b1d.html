Once the graph is constructed, the algorithm works by refining the clusters iteratively on a sequence of steps. At the first step, the clusters are determined  by setting \(\Gamma\) to the lowest value (i.e. \(1.2 \)), resulting in the highest level of coarsening (cf. Figure \ref{394885}b). Then each of the formed clusters becomes a candidate for a parent grain. And on each parent grain candidate, the most likely parent orientation is calculated by finding the mode of the ODF (Orientation Distribution Function) created by composing all possible parent orientation variants for each product grain orientation which belongs to the cluster.
